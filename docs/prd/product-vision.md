# Product Vision
## Rubric Grading Engine

**One-line summary:**
> We help teachers improve student writing by turning grading into a continuous, intelligent instruction system.

---

## The Problem

Teachers are stuck in a low-leverage loop:

- Spend hours grading essays
- Provide feedback students rarely apply
- Have no visibility into real skill gaps
- Guess what to reteach next

**Result:** High effort, low instructional impact, limited student improvement.

The root issue is not that grading is slow — it's that grading is disconnected from instruction.

---

## The Vision

Turn grading into a continuous, data-driven instruction cycle.

**Instead of:**
> Grade → done

**We create:**
> Evaluate → Diagnose → Prioritize → Act → Improve

This loop runs automatically, every assignment, at classroom scale.

---

## Core Loop

```
Student writes
      ↓
AI evaluates against rubric
      ↓
System identifies skill gaps
      ↓
Students grouped by need
      ↓
Teacher gets prioritized actions
      ↓
Teacher approves targeted practice
      ↓
Student improves
      ↓
System surfaces new patterns for teacher review
```

---

## Core Pillars

### 1. Evaluation Engine
- Rubric-based grading with per-criterion scoring
- Feedback grounded in the student's actual writing
- Transparent reasoning, fully editable by the teacher

**Principle: Trust-first AI — show the work, allow overrides.**

---

### 2. Student Intelligence Layer
- Persistent skill profiles per student
- Tracks writing ability over time across assignments
- Surfaces strengths, gaps, and trends

**Students are not essays. They are evolving systems.**

---

### 3. Prioritization Engine
- Ranks which students need attention most urgently
- Auto-groups students by shared skill gaps
- Produces a teacher "worklist" — not a dashboard to explore, but actions to take

**Every insight leads to a next step.**

---

### 4. Instruction Engine
- Recommends mini-lessons, targeted exercises, and interventions
- Supports re-submission and iterative improvement cycles
- Connects diagnosis to classroom practice

**Turns insight into action.**

---

### 5. Automation Layer
- Grades at scale with consistent rubric application
- Flags edge cases and confidence thresholds for teacher review
- Suggests next instructional steps

**Human-in-the-loop — not human-replaced.**

---

## Key Insight

This is **not** an AI grader.

This is **a decision system for teaching writing.**

The grading is the entry point. The value is everything that happens after.

---

## Target Users

**Primary:** Middle and high school English teachers

**Expansion:**
- Writing-intensive subjects (social studies, science)
- College writing instructors
- Curriculum coaches and instructional leaders

---

## Value Proposition

**For teachers:**
- Save 5–10 hours per week on grading
- Know exactly who needs help and what to teach
- Reduce grading fatigue and second-guessing

**For students:**
- Faster, more specific feedback
- Targeted improvement paths
- Visible skill growth over time

**For schools:**
- Consistent rubric application across classrooms
- Aggregate insight into writing skill trends
- Evidence base for instructional decisions

---

## Human-in-the-Loop Design

Every consequential action in the system requires explicit teacher approval. This is not a policy constraint — it is a core design principle that shapes every feature.

**What the system does without teacher input:**
- Analyzes essays and generates scores, justifications, and feedback
- Identifies skill gaps, groups students, and detects trends
- Prepares recommendations and surfaces them for review

**What the system never does without teacher approval:**
- Records or finalizes a grade
- Shares feedback or grades with students
- Assigns exercises, prompts, or interventions to students
- Sends any communication on the teacher's behalf
- Locks, closes, or transitions an assignment to a new state

The AI's role is to prepare. The teacher's role is to decide.

---

## Product Principles

1. **Workflow-first** — Fit into how teachers already work. Do not require a new system of record.
2. **Trust over automation** — Show reasoning, allow overrides, never hide the AI's work.
3. **Action over insight** — Every diagnosis must connect to a concrete next step.
4. **Speed matters** — Results in seconds. Waiting kills adoption.
5. **Teacher control** — AI assists. The teacher decides. Always.

---

## Non-Goals

These are explicitly out of scope for the core product:

- Replacing the LMS (Google Classroom, Canvas, etc.)
- Generating essays or writing for students
- Automated grade submission without teacher review
- Behavioral or social-emotional tracking
- Universal subject coverage beyond writing-focused assignments

---

## Strategic Position

| Category | Position |
|---|---|
| Replaces | Manual grading tools, single-rubric scoring apps |
| Augments | LMS platforms (Google Classroom, Canvas) |
| Outperforms | Generic AI toolkits (MagicSchool, etc.) on depth and workflow integration |
| Owns | Writing-specific decision-making, skill diagnosis, and instructional prioritization |

The strategic moat is not the AI grading — it is the **closed feedback loop between evaluation and instruction.**

---

## Phased Roadmap

### Phase 1 — Foundation
Best-in-class rubric-based grading with transparent, editable AI feedback.

### Phase 2 — Workflow
Academic integrity checks, confidence-based review, assignment management, regrade handling, and media feedback.

### Phase 3 — Student Intelligence
Persistent skill profiles, longitudinal tracking, class-level writing trends, writing process visibility.

### Phase 4 — Prioritization & Instruction
Teacher worklist, student grouping by skill gap, urgency ranking, mini-lesson recommendations, targeted practice, re-submission workflows.

### Phase 5 — Closed Loop
Fully connected cycle from assignment to feedback to instruction to improvement — teacher-directed at every step, with agents handling preparation and surfacing.

---

## Long-Term Vision

Over time, this becomes the intelligence layer on top of writing instruction — not a tool teachers open to check grades, but a system that continuously runs beneath the work of teaching and surfaces exactly what to do next.

> The system that runs writing instruction inside a classroom.
